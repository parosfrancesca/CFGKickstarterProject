THINGS TO DO

MUST SEE PLACES IN JAPAN.

  • KYOTO

    Embark on a journey to Kyoto, the enchanting former capital of Japan, celebrated globally for its sophisticated culture, delectable cuisine, and the irresistible allure of rural Japan. Every year, Kyoto City beckons millions of eager travelers, both local and international, inviting them to immerse themselves in the rich tapestry of traditional Japanese culture. Join the pilgrimage to Kyoto and discover the magic that captivates hearts from around the world.

  • TOKYO

    Indulge in Tokyo's unparalleled fusion of traditional and contemporary Japanese culture, an experience that stands unrivaled among all cities in Japan. Journey through time as you explore ancient temples and shrines, while simultaneously diving into the cutting-edge realms of futuristic technology and vibrant pop culture. Tokyo promises an extraordinary tapestry of cultural encounters that will leave you enchanted and captivated. Immerse yourself in this unique blend, and let the city unveil its kaleidoscope of wonders, inviting you on a journey like no other.

  • OSAKA

    Explore Osaka's gastronomic landscape, where the legacy of centuries meets the avant-garde, inviting you on a delectable journey of flavors and traditions.

  • MOUNT FUJI

    Explore the spiritual allure of Mount Fuji as you discover the harmonious blend of natural beauty and cultural significance that makes this landmark an essential destination on your journey through Japan.

  • ARASHIYAMA

    Arashiyama, a haven of shrines and temples seamlessly integrated with nature, beckons exploration. Among them, Tenryuji stands out for its renowned garden, Sogenchi Teien. Recognized as Japan's inaugural Special Historical Scenic Spot, the garden's seasonal transformations unveil captivating landscapes that leave a lasting impression. Immerse yourself in the beauty of Arashiyama's cultural and natural treasures, where each visit to Sogenchi Teien promises a unique and unforgettable experience.

  • KINKAKU-JI TEMPLE

    Explore the enchanting Kinkaku-Ji, known as "the Golden Pavilion," with the Japan Rail Pass. Adorned with gold leaf on its top two stories, this temple casts a luminous reflection in the Mirror Pond. Nestled at the base of Kinugasa Hill, it offers a serene retreat for peaceful strolls and moments of meditation. Immerse yourself in the golden allure of Kinkaku-Ji on your Japanese journey.
